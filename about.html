The National Democratic Institute International Leaders Forum Milwaukee - July 2020

WHAT IS THE INTERNATIONAL LEADERS FORUM? The National Democratic Institute (NDI) has organized an International Leaders Forum (ILF) at every Democratic convention since 1984 as a way for international visitors to observe the convention proceedings and take part in a series of bipartisan panel discussions on the U.S. political process, foreign and domestic policy issues, and media coverage of the presidential race. The visitors also have a rare opportunity to exchange ideas informally with their counterparts from around the world while getting a unique perspective on an American national political convention. In 2016, more than 400 international leaders from over 100 countries traveled to Philadelphia, Pennsylvania to take part in ILF.

WHAT IS THE NATIONAL DEMOCRATIC INSTITUTE? NDI (www.ndi.org) is a non-governmental organization that responds to the worldwide quest for civic participation, open and competitive political systems, and representative and accountable government. Since its founding in 1983, NDI has responded to the aspirations of people around the world to live in democratic societies that recognize and promote fundamental human rights

WHEN AND WHERE IS ILF? The 2020 International Leaders Forum will be held July 13-16 during the Democratic National Convention in Milwaukee, Wisconsin. During the day, ILF panel sessions and events will take place at a centralized location in Milwaukee. Every evening NDI will host a hospitality center for ILF participants close to the Fiserv Forum.

WHO IS INVITED? Invitees include current and former heads of state, speakers of parliament, legislators, leaders of political parties, government ministers, ambassadors and democratic activists from over 100 countries. Invitees represent ruling and opposition parties in both established and emerging democracies as well as representatives of reform movements in nations where democratic governance has not taken hold. In its nonpartisan programs to support democratic institutions and societies worldwide, NDI has worked with many of the ILF participants.

HOW IS ILF FUNDED AND HOW CAN YOU BE INVOLVED? ILF is paid for entirely with private donations. Participants pay their own airfare, lodging and other expenses. NDI welcomes the participation of private corporations in ILF and will provide a number of sponsorship opportunities during the event. Sponsors meet ILF participants, and attend daily high level policy symposiums and the Convention proceedings. Sponsor benefits include having the name and logo of the corporation highlighted in written and electronic materials, and small or side event hosting opportunities.
